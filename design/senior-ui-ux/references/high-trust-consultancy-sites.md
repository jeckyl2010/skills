# High-trust consultancy / professional-services site review notes

Use this reference when reviewing or shaping a senior independent consultancy, boutique agency, or expert-services website where the buying motion is trust-first and low-volume/high-value.

## Core review lens

These sites should optimize for:
- credibility in under 10 seconds
- clear buyer fit, not broad traffic capture
- calm confidence over sales theatrics
- fast scannability for senior decision-makers
- low-friction contact paths

## Common failure modes

- Strong writing but weak web scannability
- Repeating the same positioning message across Home / Services / About instead of giving each page a distinct job
- Long testimonial walls that contain excellent proof but hide it inside oversized quotes
- Service cards that describe capability but not "when to hire" or "what outcome this creates"
- Over-investing in polish while under-supporting buyer decision-making

## Recommended page roles

- Home: value proposition + immediate trust + clear next paths
- Services: when to hire, what problems are solved, what outcome to expect
- About: how the person works, why their judgment is credible, human trust signal
- Testimonials: summarized proof first, full proof second
- Contact: remove friction, explain what happens next, make the first step feel easy

## High-value improvements

1. Improve scannability before adding more copy
2. Turn long testimonials into excerpts / highlighted proof / progressive disclosure
3. Reframe services around buyer situations and outcomes
4. Add explicit "reasons to contact" cues
5. Reduce repeated positioning language across pages

## Useful prompts when reviewing

- Can a buyer tell in 10–15 seconds whether this person is relevant to their problem?
- Does each page carry a distinct decision-support role?
- Are trust signals sequenced well, or scattered?
- Is the site stronger as a profile than as a conversion asset?
- Are the best proof points surfaced as excerpts, not buried in full-length text?
