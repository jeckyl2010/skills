# Consultancy proof pages: scan-first trust structure

Use these notes when reviewing or revising consultancy, architecture, or senior-expert marketing pages where the site needs to establish credibility quickly without sounding salesy.

Key lessons from this session
- Strong raw testimonials are not enough if the page opens as a wall of text.
- The right structure is usually:
  1. short trust signals
  2. featured pull quotes / excerpts
  3. full recommendations behind progressive disclosure
- Buyers scan first for proof themes, not for full-length endorsements.
- Service pages should help a buyer identify when to hire, not just enumerate categories.
- Repetition across homepage, services, and about weakens differentiation even when the copy quality is high.

Useful review prompts
- Can the user understand the main proof story in under 10 seconds?
- Are there 2–4 strong trust signals visible before long-form reading begins?
- Does each page carry a distinct job, or are the same messages repeated everywhere?
- Is the contact path specific enough for a hesitant buyer to self-identify?

Good patterns
- Signal cards with short outcome-oriented proof
- Pull quotes extracted from long testimonials
- 'Read full recommendation' disclosure for long LinkedIn-style endorsements
- Framing service sections around 'when relevant' and 'what outcome it creates'

Avoid
- Full-length testimonials stacked one after another at page load
- Over-abstract hero rewrites when the user already prefers a sharper concrete line
- Dense tag clouds that feel like taxonomy instead of buyer guidance
